## 1. What is changing for SSBCI effective 1/1/2027

SSBCI are non-primarily-health-related supplemental benefits (food/produce, utilities, rent, transportation, pet supplies, etc.) that MA plans may offer **only to enrollees who are "chronically ill,"** under 42 CFR 422.102. The CY2027 final rule does **not** eliminate SSBCI, but tightens administration in ways that will shrink who actually receives them and how easily benefits can be spent:

1. **Eligibility must be determined by objective criteria, not self-attestation.** Plans must use an objective process to demonstrate an enrollee meets the chronically-ill standard. Rule language (Section II.B.5): CMS is *"strengthen[ing] the administration of Special Supplemental Benefits for the Chronically Ill (SSBCI) by increasing transparency and clarifying eligibility requirements ... we are finalizing this provision as proposed."*

2. **Public posting of eligibility criteria (codified).** Plans must post their **plan-developed SSBCI eligibility criteria on their public-facing website**, including a *"requirement to make plan-developed SSBCI eligibility criteria publicly available."* This makes the gating criteria transparent and enforceable — and harder to apply loosely.

3. **Bibliography / written-evidence standard remains in force (§ 422.102(f)(3)).** Any SSBCI item or service must be supported *"through relevant acceptable evidence that the item has a reasonable expectation of improving or maintaining the health or overall function of a chronically ill enrollee."* The rule confirms this applies even to newly clarified categories (e.g., compliant hemp-derived products).

4. **Debit/"flex" card administration is codified and constrained.** Cards must be *"electronically linked to plan-covered items and services through a real-time identification mechanism to verify eligibility ... at the point of sale,"* must be **limited to the specific plan year**, and plans must **disclose all benefit conditions and eligible items** and maintain an alternative payment method if the card malfunctions. Net effect: cards can only be used on pre-approved items at participating merchants — fewer dollars usable for general groceries/utilities/rent at the member's discretion.

5. **Cannabis / illegal-product exclusion clarified.** Products illegal under federal **or** state law cannot qualify as SSBCI; only compliant hemp-derived products may, and only if they meet the § 422.102(f)(3) evidence test.

6. **What CMS did NOT finalize (relevant context):** CMS **declined** to prohibit marketing the dollar value of supplemental benefits, and **removed** the mid-year "unused supplemental benefits" notification requirement. So plans keep advertising card value but face **less obligation to remind members to use benefits** before they expire.

**Definition of "chronically ill enrollee"** (the gate for all SSBCI). An enrollee qualifies only if they meet **all three**:
- has one or more **comorbid and medically complex chronic conditions** that is life-threatening or significantly limits overall health/function;
- has a **high risk of hospitalization** or other adverse health outcomes; **and**
- **requires intensive care coordination.**

## 2. Who loses eligibility / what gets restricted — real-world impact

**Who is squeezed:**
- **Members who never truly met the chronically-ill bar** but were enrolled under loose/self-attested screening. Objective-criteria + public-posting enforcement will **disqualify or fail to re-qualify** a meaningful share of current SSBCI recipients at CY2027 renewal.
- **Members whose plans drop or narrow SSBCI/flex-card offerings** entirely in CY2027 because the tightened compliance, documentation, and card-integrity burden makes the benefit costlier to operate. This is the larger expected effect: not just individual disqualification, but **whole plans reducing or eliminating grocery/flex-card benefits.**

**What gets restricted even for those who keep a card:**
- **Where and what** the card can buy is locked to plan-approved items/merchants via real-time point-of-sale verification — **no more broad "spend anywhere on food/utilities" flexibility.**
- **Year-locked balances + no mid-year reminders** → more **forfeited benefits**.

**Practical member harm** (the population this directory serves — KS + MO MA members losing SSBCI/flex/grocery-card benefits effective 1/1/2027):
- Loss of monthly **grocery/produce card** → food insecurity.
- Loss of **utility/rent assistance** funded through flex allowances → arrears, shutoff, eviction risk.
- Loss of **transportation/gas** support → missed medical appointments, isolation.
- Loss of **pet-food/pet-supply** allowance → pressure on fixed incomes, risk of pet surrender.
- Loss of **OTC/health-spending** flexibility that members had been stretching toward **prescriptions** and basic needs.

These are predominantly **low-income, chronically ill, fixed-income seniors** — the exact people least able to absorb a sudden benefit cut, which is why a community-resource backfill directory is warranted.
